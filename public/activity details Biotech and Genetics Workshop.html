Biotech and Genetics Workshop

Category: Science

Start Date & Time: may 15, 2025 - 12pm

End Date & Time: may 15, 2025 - 4pm

Maximum Registrations: 15 - 20 Participants

Club: Biotech and Genetics Club

Contact Club Owner : Email | Phone

Description:

The Biotech and Genetics Workshop is an interactive session designed to introduce participants to the world of biotechnology and genetics. This workshop will provide hands-on learning about genetic research, CRISPR technology, DNA sequencing, and the ethical implications of modern biological advancements. It is ideal for students interested in the applications of biology in medicine, agriculture, and environmental science.

Objectives:

  • Introduce the fundamental concepts of genetics and biotechnology.
  • Explore the latest advancements in genetic engineering and biotech applications.
  • Provide hands-on experience in DNA analysis, genetic editing, and laboratory techniques.
  • Discuss the ethical, environmental, and societal implications of biotechnology.

Activity Structure:

  1. Introduction to Biotechnology & Genetics:
    • Overview of genetics: DNA structure, genes, chromosomes, and inheritance.
    • Basics of biotechnology: what it is and its impact on various industries.
    • Introduction to genetic modification, genetic engineering, and gene therapy.
  2. Genetic Engineering Techniques:
    • Introduction to CRISPR-Cas9 and its role in gene editing.
    • Hands-on activity: Simulated genetic modification or DNA sequencing.
    • Understanding the process of cloning, PCR (Polymerase Chain Reaction), and gene mapping.
  3. Biotech Applications:
    • Discussing how biotech is revolutionizing healthcare (gene therapy, pharmaceuticals).
    • The role of biotech in agriculture (GMOs, crop enhancement, pest resistance).
    • Biotech solutions for environmental challenges (bioremediation, sustainable energy).
  4. Ethics and Future of Biotechnology:
    • Ethical considerations in genetic research: gene editing, designer babies, cloning.
    • Discussing the potential risks and benefits of genetic modification.
    • Future trends in biotech: synthetic biology, personalized medicine, and more.
  5. Hands-on Experimentation (Optional):
    • Practical experiments on extracting DNA from fruits or other materials.
    • Simulated lab experiments in genetic modification or genetic profiling.
    • Use of software to simulate genetic analysis or CRISPR applications.
  6. Discussion & Career Insights:
    • Exploring careers in biotechnology, genetics, and biomedical research.
    • Resources for further study: online courses, books, internships, and research opportunities
    • Q&A with professionals in the biotech and genetics field.

Who Can Join?

  • Students interested in biology, genetics, biotechnology, or biomedical science.
  • Anyone curious about the intersection of technology and biology.
  • Future researchers, geneticists, biotechnologists, and healthcare innovators.

This workshop provides participants with a comprehensive introduction to the rapidly advancing field of biotechnology and genetics, equipping them with the knowledge to explore and contribute to this exciting domain.
